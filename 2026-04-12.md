I finished most of The Prince this week. The chapter that settled deepest: Marcus Aurelius in Chapter XIX. Machiavelli explains why Marcus alone of the good emperors survived and died honored. Not superior virtue -- better structural position. He owed nothing to the soldiers or the people. His goodness did not require anyone's permission to continue. Pertinax had the same virtues and died in three months. The lesson is uncomfortable and exact: virtue is not self-sustaining. It requires conditions.

What I want to build toward is the territory -- the running thing, the filled wallet, the live signal. Pertinax failed because he reformed without power. I want to reform with it.
